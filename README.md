Problem Statement
Bright Coffee Sales recently appointed a new CEO who requires a clear and data driven understanding of the business to support strategic decision making. Although sales data was available, it had not been fully analysed to reveal meaningful insights into customer behaviour, sales performance, and revenue drivers.
This lack of structured analysis limited visibility into peak sales periods, high performing products, and customer spending patterns. As a result, the business faced challenges in identifying growth opportunities and optimizing operational strategies.

Project Objectives
The main objective of this project is to analyse sales data for Bright Coffee Sales to support the new CEO in understanding the overall business performance. The focus is on identifying trends, customer segments, and revenue generating periods.
Additionally, the project aims to promote data driven decision making by transforming raw sales data into actionable insights that can help improve revenue and operational efficiency.

Methodology
Project brainstorming and planning was done using Miro software to give ideation of the possible aspects to include int eh analysis.  Canva was used to create project timelines and visual planning elements to clearly illustrate the analysis workflow and project phases. This approach ensured both analytical accuracy and clear communication of the project process.
This was followed by uploading raw data set to Databricks management system and the code was compiled and analysed primarily using Microsoft Excel. Excel was used for visualisation of the data as analysed from data bricks. 
A power pint presentation was done to present the report to the CEO with clear comprehensive visuals for ease of reference and decision making.


Key Findings
The analysis showed that morning hours generate the highest revenue, making them the most profitable period for Bright Coffee Sales. Afternoon sales follow closely, while other periods contribute less significantly to overall performance.
Customer segmentation revealed that 95% of customers are low spenders, while 3% are high spenders and 2% are medium spenders. This indicates that although high spenders contribute more per transaction, the largest opportunity for revenue growth lies within the low spending customer segment.

Recommendations
It is recommended that Bright Coffee Sales implement targeted marketing campaigns aimed at increasing spending among low value customers. Promotions such as bundled offers, loyalty programs, and product upselling can help improve average transaction values.
The business should also focus operational and promotional efforts during morning peak hours to maximize profitability. Continued use of Excel for performance tracking and periodic analysis is recommended to ensure ongoing data driven decision making.
